Charter of the Anarchy Network Naming Authority (ANNA)

Established under the .nvos.dev Root Domain System

We, the founding members of the global community served by the .nvos.dev namespace, recognizing the fundamental importance of a stable, secure, and universally accessible domain name system for international cooperation and digital sovereignty, hereby establish the Anarchy Network Naming Authority (ANNA) as an independent, neutral institution dedicated to the equitable administration of domain registration services. In acknowledgment of the interconnected nature of our digital infrastructure and the need for technical expertise in maintaining global network stability, we commit to the principles of transparency, fairness, and collaborative governance while ensuring the continued security and operational integrity of the root system.

Article I: Mission and Purpose

Section 1.1 - Primary Mission

The Anarchy Network Naming Authority (ANNA) shall serve as the neutral administrator of the .nvos.dev top-level domain, ensuring equitable access to domain registration services for all recognized sovereign entities, international organizations, and qualified applicants without discrimination based on political ideology, economic system, or territorial disputes.

Section 1.2 - Core Functions

ANNA shall:

  • Evaluate and approve applications for second-level domains under .nvos.dev
  • Maintain the technical infrastructure necessary for global domain resolution
  • Establish and enforce uniform standards for domain registration and management
  • Provide dispute resolution mechanisms for domain-related conflicts
  • Ensure the long-term stability and security of the root namespace

Article II: Fundamental Principles

Section 2.1 - Neutrality and Non-Interference

ANNA shall maintain strict political neutrality in all domain registration decisions, treating all applicants equally regardless of their governmental structure, economic policies, or international relations status.

Section 2.2 - Transparency and Accountability

All policy decisions, registration procedures, and administrative actions shall be conducted with maximum transparency, subject to legitimate security considerations and operational requirements that may necessitate confidential handling of certain technical information.

Section 2.3 - Technical Independence

ANNA shall maintain full technical autonomy in matters relating to system architecture, security protocols, and infrastructure management, ensuring decisions are made solely on technical merit and operational necessity.

Section 2.4 - Stability and Security

The paramount obligation of ANNA shall be the preservation of system stability, security, and global accessibility, which may require immediate protective measures when credible threats to infrastructure integrity are identified.

Article III: Governance Structure

Section 3.1 - Organizational Independence

ANNA shall operate as an autonomous institution, with governance structures designed to ensure independence from any single national or political influence while maintaining necessary operational flexibility.

Section 3.2 - Technical Advisory Council

A Technical Advisory Council, composed of qualified experts in network administration and cybersecurity, shall provide guidance on technical matters. The composition and selection procedures for this Council shall be established through separate implementing regulations, with due consideration for technical expertise and operational requirements.

Section 3.3 - Administrative Authority

Day-to-day operations shall be managed by an Administrative Authority responsible for implementing policies, processing applications, and maintaining system security. The Administrative Authority shall exercise independent judgment in all matters relating to technical operations and security protocols.

Article IV: Registration Procedures

Section 4.1 - Application Process

Domain registration shall follow standardized procedures designed to ensure fair evaluation while maintaining necessary security screening. All applications shall be reviewed according to established criteria, technical requirements, and security assessments.

Section 4.2 - Evaluation Criteria

Applications shall be evaluated based on:

  • Legitimacy of the applying entity
  • Technical capacity to maintain proper domain administration
  • Compliance with established security protocols
  • Absence of conflicts with existing registrations or reserved names
  • Conformity with operational stability requirements

Section 4.3 - Review Timeline

ANNA shall endeavor to process applications within reasonable timeframes, though complex applications requiring enhanced security review, technical assessment, or coordination with operational partners may require extended evaluation periods as determined necessary by the Administrative Authority.

Section 4.4 - Appeal Process

Applicants may request reconsideration of negative decisions through established channels, though final determinations on matters involving technical security, infrastructure stability, or operational requirements shall rest with the Administrative Authority.

Article V: Operational Standards

Section 5.1 - Technical Requirements

All registered domains must comply with established technical standards designed to ensure system-wide compatibility, security, and performance. Non-compliance with technical requirements may result in suspension of services pending remediation.

Section 5.2 - Security Protocols

ANNA shall maintain comprehensive security protocols to protect against cyber threats, unauthorized access, and system disruption. Implementation of security measures may require temporary restrictions on access or functionality when credible threats are identified.

Section 5.3 - Infrastructure Protection

The protection of core infrastructure shall take precedence over individual domain services when conflicts arise. ANNA reserves the right to implement protective measures, including temporary service modifications, to preserve overall system integrity.

Article VI: Enforcement and Remedial Actions

Section 6.1 - Compliance Monitoring

ANNA shall conduct regular assessments of domain compliance with established standards, security requirements, and operational protocols. Non-compliance issues shall be addressed through appropriate remedial procedures.

Section 6.2 - Suspension and Revocation

Domains may be suspended or revoked for:

  • Violation of technical standards or security protocols
  • Compromise of system security or stability
  • Use in activities that threaten infrastructure integrity
  • Failure to maintain compliance with evolving security requirements
  • Non-cooperation with legitimate security investigations

Section 6.3 - Emergency Powers

In circumstances involving imminent threats to system security, public safety, or infrastructure stability, the Administrative Authority may take immediate protective action, including temporary suspension of services, pending full review through established procedures.

Section 6.4 - Due Process

While ANNA is committed to fair procedures, the exigencies of maintaining global infrastructure security may require expedited processes when delay would compromise system integrity or public safety.

Article VII: Information Management

Section 7.1 - Records Maintenance

ANNA shall maintain comprehensive records of all registration activities, technical operations, and administrative decisions in accordance with established archival standards and operational security requirements.

Section 7.2 - Information Sharing

While committed to transparency, certain operational information may be classified to protect infrastructure security, ongoing investigations, or competitive technical advantages essential to system stability.

Section 7.3 - Confidentiality Protocols

Technical details regarding security vulnerabilities, operational procedures, and infrastructure configurations may be subject to confidentiality restrictions when disclosure could compromise system security or operational effectiveness.

Article VIII: Amendment and Interpretation

Section 8.1 - Charter Amendments

This Charter may be amended through procedures that ensure broad consultation while maintaining operational continuity and security. Amendments affecting technical operations or security protocols may be expedited when necessary for infrastructure protection.

Section 8.2 - Interpretation Authority

Questions of Charter interpretation, particularly regarding technical requirements and security protocols, shall be resolved by the Administrative Authority in consultation with the Technical Advisory Council, with decisions guided by the paramount importance of system stability and security.

Section 8.3 - Operational Flexibility

This Charter shall be interpreted to provide necessary operational flexibility for ANNA to fulfill its technical mission and security responsibilities, recognizing that rigid procedures may conflict with the dynamic requirements of infrastructure management.

Article IX: Final Provisions

Section 9.1 - Effective Date

This Charter shall take effect immediately upon adoption and shall govern all operations of ANNA under the .nvos.dev namespace.

Section 9.2 - Continuity of Operations

ANNA shall ensure uninterrupted service delivery during any transitional periods, organizational changes, or emergency situations, with operational continuity taking precedence over administrative procedures when conflicts arise.

Section 9.3 - International Cooperation

ANNA shall maintain cooperative relationships with all legitimate stakeholders while preserving its technical independence and security responsibilities.
