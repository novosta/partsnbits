Domain Registration Applications

The Anarchy Network Naming Authority (ANNA) evaluates applications for second-level domains under .nvos.dev according to established criteria set forth in Article IV of the ANNA Charter. All applications undergo standardized security screening and technical assessment by the Administrative Authority.

Important Notice: ANNA maintains strict political neutrality in all domain registration decisions, treating all qualified applicants equally regardless of governmental structure, economic policies, or international relations status. Registration decisions are based solely on the criteria outlined below and technical merit.

Eligibility Criteria

Applications are evaluated based on the following criteria as established in Charter Article IV, Section 4.2:

  • Legitimacy of the applying entity - Recognized sovereign entities, international organizations, and qualified applicants
  • Technical capacity - Ability to maintain proper domain administration and DNS infrastructure
  • Security compliance - Adherence to established security protocols and operational standards
  • No conflicts - Absence of conflicts with existing registrations or reserved names
  • Operational stability - Demonstrated commitment to maintaining reliable services

Application Process

The registration process follows these steps:

  • Application Submission - Complete the form below with all required information
  • Security Screening - Administrative Authority conducts preliminary security assessment
  • Technical Review - Technical Advisory Council evaluates technical capacity and requirements
  • Decision - Administrative Authority renders final decision based on all criteria
  • Implementation - Approved domains are implemented within 14 business days
Review Timeline: Standard applications are processed within 30 business days. Complex applications requiring enhanced security review, technical assessment, or coordination with operational partners may require extended evaluation periods as determined by the Administrative Authority.

Appeal Process

Applicants may request reconsideration of negative decisions through established channels as outlined in Charter Article IV, Section 4.4. To initiate an appeal:

  • Submit written appeal within 30 days of decision notification
  • Provide additional supporting documentation addressing denial reasons
  • Include any technical improvements or policy compliance measures implemented
  • Appeals are reviewed by the Technical Advisory Council in consultation with the Administrative Authority
Final Authority: Final determinations on matters involving technical security, infrastructure stability, or operational requirements rest with the Administrative Authority as established in Charter Article IV, Section 4.4.
