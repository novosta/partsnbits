ANNA Security Framework

The Anarchy Network Naming Authority operates under a comprehensive security framework established by Charter Articles V, VI, and VII. This framework prioritizes the protection of critical infrastructure while ensuring operational continuity and stakeholder security.

Mandate: The paramount obligation of ANNA is the preservation of system stability, security, and global accessibility. This may require immediate protective measures when credible threats to infrastructure integrity are identified (Charter Article II, Section 2.4).

Core Security Principles

  • Infrastructure Protection: Core infrastructure takes precedence over individual domain services when conflicts arise
  • Threat Prevention: Proactive monitoring and assessment of potential security vulnerabilities
  • Rapid Response: Emergency powers enable immediate protective action when threats are detected
  • Operational Security: Classified information protocols protect sensitive technical details
  • Compliance Monitoring: Regular assessments ensure adherence to security requirements

Domain Security Requirements

All registered domains under .nvos.dev must comply with established technical standards designed to ensure system-wide compatibility, security, and performance as mandated by Charter Article V, Section 5.1.

Mandatory Security Protocols

  • DNS Security Extensions (DNSSEC) implementation required for all domains
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Incident response procedures and emergency contact protocols
  • Compliance with evolving security requirements and updates
  • Cooperation with legitimate security investigations and assessments
Compliance Notice: Non-compliance with technical requirements may result in suspension of services pending remediation. Domain operators must maintain current security certifications and respond to security directives within specified timeframes.

Threat Response and Emergency Powers

ANNA maintains comprehensive threat response capabilities under Charter Article VI, Section 6.3, enabling immediate protective action when circumstances involve imminent threats to system security, public safety, or infrastructure stability.

OPERATIONAL SECURITY: Specific threat response procedures and emergency protocols are classified under Charter Article VII, Section 7.3. Disclosure of detailed operational procedures could compromise system security and operational effectiveness.

Response Capabilities

  • Immediate Suspension: Temporary suspension of compromised domains pending security review
  • Traffic Redirection: Emergency rerouting of DNS traffic to maintain service continuity
  • Isolation Protocols: Quarantine of affected infrastructure segments
  • Stakeholder Coordination: Rapid communication with affected domain operators and authorities
  • Forensic Analysis: Comprehensive investigation of security incidents

Compliance Monitoring and Enforcement

ANNA conducts regular assessments of domain compliance with established standards, security requirements, and operational protocols as specified in Charter Article VI, Section 6.1. Non-compliance issues are addressed through appropriate remedial procedures.

Assessment Framework

  • Quarterly Reviews: Comprehensive security and compliance assessments
  • Continuous Monitoring: Automated surveillance of domain performance and security
  • Incident Analysis: Post-incident reviews and improvement recommendations
  • Stakeholder Reporting: Regular compliance status reports to domain operators
Enforcement Authority: Domains may be suspended or revoked for violation of technical standards, compromise of system security, use in activities threatening infrastructure integrity, failure to maintain compliance, or non-cooperation with security investigations (Charter Article VI, Section 6.2).
